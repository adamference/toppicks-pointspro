Credit Card Basics

A credit card is a revolving line of credit issued by a bank or financial institution. When used responsibly, it helps build your credit history, offers purchase protection, and can earn rewards like cash back, travel points, or statement credits.

Key Terms to Know

  • APR (Annual Percentage Rate) — The yearly interest rate charged on balances you carry past the grace period.
  • Annual Fee — A yearly charge some cards impose for premium perks and rewards.
  • Credit Utilization — The percentage of your available credit you're using. Keeping this under 30% helps your score.
  • Minimum Payment — The smallest amount you can pay each month without incurring a penalty.
  • Grace Period — The window between your statement closing date and payment due date when no interest accrues on new purchases.
