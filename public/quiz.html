How Our Card Matching Works

Our recommendation engine uses the information you provide — your estimated credit score, financial goals, and timeline — to filter through offers from over 40 partner issuers. We rank results based on approval likelihood, reward value, and overall cardholder satisfaction.

Unlike generic comparison tools, PointsPro tailors recommendations to your specific profile. Someone with excellent credit will see very different results from someone rebuilding their score, because the best card for you depends on where you are financially.

Tips Before You Apply

Getting the most out of a new credit card starts before you even submit an application. Here are a few steps that improve your chances and help you choose wisely.

Check Your Credit Report First

You're entitled to a free credit report from each of the three major bureaus once per year through AnnualCreditReport.com. Review yours for errors — incorrect balances, accounts you don't recognize, or outdated negative marks. Disputing errors can raise your score before you apply.

Keep Your Utilization Low

Credit utilization — the percentage of your available credit you're using — is one of the biggest factors in your score. Try to keep it below 30%, and ideally under 10%, in the months before applying for a new card.

Don't Apply for Too Many Cards at Once

Each application generates a hard inquiry. Spacing applications out by at least 3–6 months reduces the impact on your score and avoids looking like a risk to issuers.
